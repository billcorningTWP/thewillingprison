By Bill Corning

The Book

It's supposed to be temporary — a shelter to retreat, regroup, recover.
But some men never leave.

The walls become the thing that traps them. The fortress becomes a Willing Prison.

This is the story of one man's forty-six years inside. But it's not really about him.

It's about the father who is present but never there. The husband whose wife reaches for him and finds only a ghost. It's about what happens when we overstay our welcome in the places we built to protect ourselves.

"The key is in your pocket. It always was.
Will you unlock the door?"

Written with dark humor, hard-won honesty, and the kind of self-deprecation that can only come from someone who has lived it — The Willing Prison is for any man who has retreated so far inside himself that he forgot there was a world waiting on the other side.

About the Author

Bill Corning

Author  ·  First Book

Bill Corning spent decades building walls — around networks, around systems, and around his own heart. A shift in health and life led him to a new path: tearing them down on the page.

He is a devoted husband, a proud father, and the richest man alive in all the ways that matter most.

The Willing Prison is his first book.
